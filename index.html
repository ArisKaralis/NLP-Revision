Welcome to Classic NLP Fundamentals

This site explores fundamental methodologies within classical Natural Language Processing (NLP). These techniques form a critical underpinning for more advanced neural approaches that you might be familiar with from your AI studies. We'll delve into statistical language modeling, text processing techniques, sequence labelling methodologies, and sparse vector representations of textual data.

Note for AI Master's Students: Understanding these classic techniques provides a strong foundation for several reasons:

  • Conceptual Building Blocks: Many modern neural NLP models have roots in or address limitations of these classic methods. Knowing the history helps in understanding the "why" behind current architectures.
  • Baselines & Simpler Solutions: For certain tasks or with limited data, classic NLP techniques can still be surprisingly effective and computationally cheaper than large neural models. They provide excellent baselines for comparison.
  • Feature Engineering Insights: While deep learning automates much feature engineering, understanding how features were manually created in classic NLP can provide intuition for designing better neural network inputs or interpreting their behavior.
  • Scalability Understanding: Many classic techniques were designed with scalability in mind for large corpora, offering lessons applicable to today's big data challenges.
